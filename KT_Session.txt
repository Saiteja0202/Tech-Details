## 🔹 Employee Benefits
Employee benefits are extra perks employers give beyond salary to improve security and living standards.

- **Example**: Imagine your company pays for your health insurance and gives you paid vacation. That’s an employee benefit.

---

## 🔹 Welfare Benefit Plan
Covers non‑retirement benefits like health, training, or legal help.

- **Medical care** → Company pays for your hospital bills.  
- **Unemployment benefits** → If you lose your job, you get temporary financial support.  
- **Day care centers** → Employer provides childcare so parents can work.  

👉 Think of it as “life support” benefits while you’re working.

---

## 🔹 Pension Benefit Plan
Covers retirement income. Defined by **ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)**.

- **Example**: Your employer sets up a pension plan so when you retire at 65, you get monthly payments.

---

## 🔹 Two Types of Pension Plans
### 1. Defined Contribution Plan
- Employer contributes a fixed amount or percentage into your account.  
- Your retirement depends on how investments perform.  
- **Example**: Mary earns $45,000. Employer contributes 5% = $2,250 into her retirement account. If investments grow, she gets more; if they lose value, she gets less.

👉 Risk is on the employee.

---

### 2. Defined Benefit Plan
- Employer promises a fixed retirement income (like a monthly pension).  
- Payments usually last until death.  
- **Example**: Employer promises $2,000/month after retirement at age 65. No matter how investments perform, Mary gets $2,000.

👉 Risk is on the employer. Protected by PBGC (Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation).

---

## 🔹 Contracts
### Participating Contract
Employer pays premiums regularly to keep annuity active.  
- **Example**: Employer pays $10M upfront, then adds more funds over time to cover retirees.

### Non‑Participating Contract
Employer pays a single lump sum.  
- **Example**: Employer pays $5M once to cover all employees’ future pensions.

---

## 🔹 Retirement Provisions
- **Normal Retirement** → Usually age 65.  
- **Early Retirement** → Retire earlier (like 55), but get smaller benefits.  
- **Delayed Retirement** → Retire later (like 70), get larger benefits.  

👉 Example: Retiring at 55 gives smaller monthly checks; retiring at 70 gives bigger ones.

---

## 🔹 Forms of Annuity
An annuity = regular payments after retirement.

- **Straight Life Annuity** → Payments stop when you die.  
- **Term Certain Annuity** → Payments for a fixed period (e.g., 10 years), even if you die earlier.  
- **Life with Refund Annuity** → If you die early, your family gets a refund of unused money.  
- **Joint & Survivor Annuity** → Payments continue to your spouse after your death.  

👉 Example: If you choose Joint & Survivor, you get $1,500/month. After you die, your spouse continues to get $750/month.

---

## 🔹 Benefit Provisions
- **Pre‑Retirement Spouse Death Benefit (PRSDB)** → If you die before retirement, spouse gets benefits.  
- **Post‑Retirement Survivor Benefit (PSSDB)** → If you die after retirement, spouse gets part of your pension.  
- **Cash‑Out** → If pension is too small, you get one lump sum instead of monthly payments.  
- **Lump‑Sum** → You choose to get all money at once.  
- **Flat Death Benefit** → Fixed amount paid to beneficiary after death (e.g., $5,000).

---

## 🔹 Distribution & Taxation
- **QDRO (Qualified Domestic Relations Order)** → Court order to split pension benefits (e.g., divorce, child support).  
- **Federal & State Tax** → Pension payments are taxable.  
- **Rollover** → Move pension money into another retirement account (like IRA) to keep tax benefits.  

👉 Example: You retire and roll over your pension into an IRA to avoid paying taxes immediately.

---

## 🔹 Other Retirement Accounts
- **IRA (Individual Retirement Account)** → Personal retirement savings with tax benefits.  
- **SEP (Simplified Employee Pension)** → Retirement plan for small businesses/self‑employed.  

---

## 🔹 Advantages & Disadvantages
### Defined Benefit (DB)
- ✅ Guaranteed lifetime income.  
- ❌ Less portable if you change jobs.  

### Defined Contribution (DC)
- ✅ Portable, flexible.  
- ❌ Risk is on employee (investments may fail).

---

## ✅ Beginner Summary
- **Welfare Benefits** = support during work life (health, vacation, training).  
- **Pension Benefits** = support after retirement (monthly income or lump sum).  
- **Defined Contribution** = employer deposits money, you bear risk.  
- **Defined Benefit** = employer promises fixed pension, employer bears risk.  
- **Annuities** = different payment styles (life, joint, refund, lump sum).  
- **Contracts** = how employer funds pensions (ongoing vs one‑time).  
- **Taxes & Rollovers** = pensions are taxable, but rollovers delay taxes.

# 🧾 Beginner-Friendly Explanation of V3 Functional Overview

## 🌐 What is V3?
- **V3** is a software product built by **ViTech**, customized for **MetLife**.
- It’s used for **group benefits administration** — basically managing pensions, annuities, and insurance benefits for groups of people (like employees in a company).
- Think of it like a **big dashboard + database** that keeps track of who gets what benefits, when, and how.

---

## 📦 Product Groups in V3
V3 handles three main types of products:

1. **Closeouts**  
   - When a company stops its pension plan, they buy a contract from MetLife to guarantee employees still get their pensions.  
   - Two types:
     - **Non-Participating**: Company pays one big lump sum → MetLife takes full responsibility.  
     - **Participating**: Company pays lump sum + ongoing premiums → they share financial risks/benefits with MetLife.

2. **Structured Settlements (SS, CGA & Lotteries)**  
   - Used when someone wins a court case or lottery. Instead of one big payout, they get **regular payments over time**.  
   - Example: A person injured in an accident gets monthly payments for medical expenses instead of one lump sum.

3. **Legacy Products**  
   - Old products MetLife no longer sells but still has to manage because customers are still entitled to benefits.  
   - Includes products from companies MetLife acquired (like NEF, MICC, GenAm).

---

## 💡 Why V3 is Useful
- Combines data from many old systems into **one modern Oracle database**.  
- Makes processes faster, cheaper, and more transparent.  
- Improves customer service (easy to find info in one place).  
- Helps MetLife expand into new markets.

---

## 👥 Business Entities in V3
V3 organizes people and organizations into **entities**:

- **Individual Class**  
  - **Person**: Any participant (employee, broker, consultant, etc.).  
  - **Member**: Employee who earns benefits.  
  - **Dependent**: Family member who gets benefits because of the member.  
  - **Beneficiary**: Person who receives benefits if the member dies.

- **Institution Class**  
  - **Plan Sponsor**: Employer or organization funding the benefits.  
  - **Other Org**: Banks, law firms, lottery companies, etc.

So V3 can track **who is involved** (employee, dependent, broker, sponsor) and their **roles**.

---

## 🔍 Searching in V3
Two ways to find information:

1. **World Search**  
   - Simple search across many fields (like Google search).  
   - Example: Search by SSN, name, tax ID, broker code.

2. **Advanced Search**  
   - More specific forms:
     - **Multi Search**: Search by SSN, certificate number, etc.  
     - **Factor Co and Name Search**: Search by factoring company and last name.

---

## 👨‍👩‍👧 Entity Examples
- **Member**: Employee who earns pension.  
- **Dependent**: Spouse or child eligible for benefits.  
- **Beneficiary**: Person who gets benefits if member dies.  
- **Plan Sponsor**: Employer who pays premiums.  
- **Broker**: Middleman who helps set up contracts.

---

## ⚖️ Structured Settlements
- **Definition**: A financial arrangement where payments are spread over time instead of one lump sum.  
- **Why**: Helps injured people or lottery winners manage money better.  
- **How it works**:
  - Insurer buys an annuity (guaranteed payment plan) from a life insurance company.  
  - Payments match the settlement agreement.  
  - Can be **unassigned** (insurer keeps obligation) or **assigned** (insurer transfers obligation to another company).

---

## 🎁 Special Settlement Types
- **Multi-Life Structured Settlement**: Covers not just the claimant but also family members.  
- **Charitable Gift Annuity (CGA)**: Donor gives money to charity → charity buys annuity → donor gets lifetime payouts.  
- **Lottery Setup**: Lottery company hires insurer to pay winners over time instead of lump sum.

---

## 🧑‍💼 Broker Processes
- **Add Broker**: Enter broker info in system.  
- **Verify Broker**: Check broker license validity.  
- **Trigger Broker Commission**: Pay broker their commission.  
- **Manual Lock-in Number**: Generate workflow ID for broker verification.

---

## 🚀 Case Activation
- Final step to start payments.  
- After premiums are received and verified, the case is activated.  
- Generates certification documents.  
- For structured settlements, a **Good Order process** ensures everything is correct before payments begin.

---

# 📝 Summary for Beginners
- **V3 = MetLife’s benefits management system.**
- Handles **Closeouts, Structured Settlements, Legacy Products.**
- Organizes people/entities into **Members, Dependents, Beneficiaries, Plan Sponsors, Brokers.**
- Provides **search tools** to find records quickly.
- Supports **special financial setups** like structured settlements, charitable annuities, and lottery payouts.
- Includes **broker workflows** and **case activation** to ensure payments are accurate and legal.
